## Acknowledging the underlying problem

The Grassmannian line-packing problem that this tool diagnoses is the same problem catalogued in the **Game of Sloanes** leaderboard. If your work concerns packings drawn from that source, please also cite it:

> Jasper, J., King, E. J., and Mixon, D. G. (2019). "Game of Sloanes: Best known packings in complex projective space." *Wavelets and Sparsity XVIII*, SPIE Proceedings 11138, 416–425. [arXiv:1907.07848](https://arxiv.org/abs/1907.07848)

The leaderboard repository is curated by John Jasper, Emily J. King, and Dustin G. Mixon at <https://github.com/gnikylime/GameofSloanes>.

The literature referenced inside the tool's *About & Theory* tab — Welch, Levenshtein, Rankin, Bukh–Cox, Strohmer–Heath, Casazza–Fickus–Mixon, Bodmann–Haas, Benedetto–Fickus, Bargmann, Conway–Hardin–Sloane, Cohn–Kumar, Renes–Blume-Kohout–Scott–Caves, and Fickus–Mixon's ETF existence tables — is the foundation that the diagnostic toolkit builds on, and any specific quantity drawn from those works should be cited at its primary source rather than via this tool.
